## Incident Response & Operations Philosophy
**Question:** How do you handle critical system failures or high-pressure incidents?
**Answer:**
* **Situation:** At Hansen Technologies, critical production incidents across our multi-million dollar enterprise SaaS portfolio were creating operational friction.
* **Task:** As Service Delivery Manager, I needed to take immediate command of critical incident triage, stabilize systems, and establish a transparent communication framework.
* **Action:** I directed live triage management for these critical production incidents, serving as the central client-facing escalation point to shield the engineering teams so they could focus entirely on resolution. Concurrently, I facilitated cross-functional alignment across technical workstreams to drive rapid stabilization. Post-incident, I isolated systemic operational bottlenecks and engineered scalable IT system runbooks.
* **Result:** This approach significantly improved our first reply time, dramatically reduced manual support volume, and established a repeatable governance model that prevented future recurrences.

**Question:** What is your philosophy on building operational frameworks?
**Answer:** Operational frameworks must perfectly balance team autonomy with rigorous executive visibility. My approach centers on constructing scalable ITSM workflows—utilizing enterprise Jira administration to build automated tracking and project controls—while embedding standardized Agile delivery principles. By combining automated tracking dashboards with clear KPI and OKR metrics, technical teams are empowered to resolve incidents predictably, while senior leadership retains total insight into portfolio health.

## Behavioral Q&A (STAR Method)
**Question:** What would you consider your biggest weakness?
**Answer:** Because I hold an AAS in CIS - Programming, my natural instinct when a high-pressure incident occurs is to dive directly into the custom code base or technical weeds to engineer a fix myself. Over time, I’ve learned to mitigate this by consciously stepping back into my leadership capacity. I focus instead on managing the operational framework, facilitating cross-functional communication, and trusting my engineering assets to execute the solution.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you had to pivot a technical strategy and communicate it to executive leadership or the C-Suite.
**Answer:**
* **Situation:** At Hansen Technologies, our regional portfolio reporting and metrics tracking were heavily manual, capping team capacity as active accounts scaled past 125+.
* **Task:** I needed to pivot our analytical strategy toward an automated framework that aligned with strategic priorities without requiring a headcount increase.
* **Action:** I architected and implemented a custom, AI-driven portfolio telemetry tracking system from scratch, utilizing generative AI workflows to automate data analysis. I translated these complex technical architectures into a clear business case and presented the strategic delivery insights directly to executive leadership.
* **Result:** The C-suite fully backed the transition, which expanded our regional operational capacity, preserved delivery predictability, and successfully provided leadership with automated delivery insights.
